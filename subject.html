Introduction

League of Legends is a MOBA, or multiplayer online battle arena, game developed by Riot Games. It was first released on October 27, 2009 and has been one of the most popular, if not the most popular PC game in the world. The game has over ten million daily players with more increasing every year. In order to succeed in this playerbase, one must have thinking skills such as decision making as well as mechanical skills such as reaction time and rapid keyboard actions. Roles

In League of Legends, there are five main roles someone can choose to play as, each with their own unique responsibilities and playstyles. These roles are the top laner, the jungler, the mid laner, the adc, and the support. The name of these roles correspond to the area of the map Summoners Rift of where the players are located for the beginning of the game in what we call "laning phase".

The fandom page for lanes can be found here.

Top

The top lane is the longest lane in the game along with having three bushes. Due to its distance covered, top laners usually play champions that have 1v1 superiority such as bruisers, duelists, and tanks. If one player gets ahead in the lane, it will be difficult to come back due to the lane length preventing the players that's behind from walking up too far to get resources. The top laner's goal is usually to win their own matchup and use their lead to help other parts of the map get ahead.

Jungler

Junglers have the most freedom within the rift. The areas in between the lanes without protection from the turrets is called the jungle. Instead of killing minions to gain gold and experience, junglers have to kill monster camps. This role requires the most decision making out of every role since they have a large amount of control in how events play out in every part of the map. This also means that junglers will have the most impact in the game since their actions can affect every other laner. Their main obective is to do surprise attacks on laners called 'ganks' in order to get their own teammates ahead.

Mid

Mid laners sit in the center of the whole map, which gives them a large amount of impact in other lanes. Champions that can traverse the rift fast and roam around are usually played in the mid lane along with mages who have attacks with long ranges and area of affect. Helping the jungler whenever a skirmish occurs is a large part in getting the team ahead as a mid laner. Roaming around to the top and bottom lanes can also help get free kills to get themselves ahead.

Adc

Adc, or attack damage carry, are usually played by marksmen champions. Their goal is to carry teamfights with insane DPS and to farm minions for gold to buy enough items for their powerspikes. These are the players that are most crucial in protecting so they can dish out damage onto the enemy team. They sit in the bottom lane with a support to provide them help and safety during laning phase.

Support

The support's role is to help the team by either providing heals and shields in the back or commencing the fight through a strong engage. They sit in the bottom lane with the adc early game in order to give opportunities for kills or protecting their own adc from the enemy team's threats. If there's a period of time where the adc is safe without them, supports are able to roam aroudn the map and provide assistance to other laners, mainly the jungler.

Champion Classes

There are 154 champions, which are playable characters, in League of Legends currently. Each champion has their own respective playstyle and functions differently in their kits. Players have classified this ocean of champions into classes that correspond to what they do best. A few of these classes are assassins, bruisers, marksmen, tanks, and control mages. There are much more than just these five, but these are some of the most common picks.

A list of every champion can be found here.

Assassins

Assassins are one of the most mobile classes in League in order to quickly reach the back line damage dealers of the enemy team. They have abilities with high burst damage to pop a damage carry and quickly retreat into the fog of war. Assassins are usually played in the mid lane and jungle, where they have the option to roam around the map and look for easy targets.

Bruisers

A combination of tankiness and consistent high damage output. Brusiers are the juggernauts that have large base stats of hp and resistances, along with kits that output crazy amounts of damage. Bruisers usually have abilities that grant healing so they can sustain longer in teamfights or in lane. They're usually played in the top lane, where they can dominate the opponent.

Marksmen

Marksmen are long range damage dealers usually carrying a ranged weapon. Instead of using their abilities to do damage, most of their damage output comes from their basic auto attacks. They usually build a ton of attack speed and critical chance to maximize their DPS. Marksmen are usually incredibly squishy, meaning they have a small health pool, and require other teammates to defend them. They're usually played in the adc role.

Tanks

Basically the same as what they are in every other game. A tank's role is to soak up damage and create engages for their own team. They build items with a ton of resistances and health to survive incoming attacks from the enemy team. Since they won't be building damage stats, their abilities usually have high base damages so they can still pose a threat to unsuspecting opponents. Tanks are usually played in the top lane or as a support.

Control Mages

Having abilities with long ranges, control mages specialize in zoning the enemy from crucial positions and dishing out consistent damage through their abilities from far distances. Control mages rely on magic damage through abilities to deal damage and they don't do much damage through basic attacks. Mana is a necessity for them, having them require resource mangement. Control mages usually have many crowd control or immobilizing abilities to catch out of position enemies. They're usually played in the mid lane.
